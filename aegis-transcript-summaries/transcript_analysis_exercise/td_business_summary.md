II. Earnings Call Summary
Credit: Reviewed credit portfolios and bolstered reserves
Macro Changes and Governance Process

"First are the macro changes, determined by our chief economist. They're not determined by me, but we have a strong governance process and we take it through our governance process."

Tariff Scenario Modeling

We did put tariffs into these scenarios:

For Canada: tariffs of 10% for six months, then those come down over a period of time
For the U.S.: tariffs of 12% for six months, then those come down over a period of time



Downside Case and Reserve Building

"Our downside case is a recessionary case. And that downside case also reflects the risks of higher tariffs. In addition to the macro, what we've done is we've looked more holistically at the book and said at higher tariffs rate what could be the impact on consumers and non-consumers and that's the way we built the overlay. So, it's really a combination of these macro plus the judgmental overlay that got us to the $500MM number [business reserve build]."
Reserve allocation strategy: Built reserves for consumer, business, and government lending. Expected bigger impact on non-retail sectors. Most of the $500MM reserve build has been allocated to business and government lending. If macro conditions deteriorate, consumer impact is also expected.

Industry-Specific Impact Assessment

"We looked at the industries that were most exposed to tariffs. What we did is we went actually within those industries borrow by borrower and assessed what is the financial impact on the borrower. So for example we'd look at revenue we'd look at cost of goods sold and we'd look at what impact that would have on the ratings of that individual."

Key Exposure Metrics:

Gross loans to industries most exposed to policy and trade uncertainty: ~9% of Total Bank gross loans
Loans to borrowers most sensitive to policy and trade actions: <1% of Total Bank gross loans
"The 9% number that we have disclosed, we went and looked on a more narrow basis saying what out of these borrowers are most sensitive to tariffs. And, we looked broadly at the financial impact, again, across the 9%, not just the smaller population. We looked at the ratings migration that could occur, and based on that ratings migration, we built our reserves. There is some for consumer because if the macro deteriorates, I think you should expect there'll be impact on the consumer as well."

Forward-Looking Reserve Philosophy

"Our reserves reflect a forward-looking view as at April 30. So, if that forward-looking view evolves we may have to build more reserves."
