8. Model Testing

Model Name: IRIS (Intelligent Research and Information System) Model ID: IRIS-001

This section documents the testing methodology and results used to validate the IRIS system. The testing focused on evaluating the system's ability to retrieve relevant information from knowledge sources and generate appropriate responses across a range of finance and accounting policy queries. Supporting test artifacts, including test questions, APG feedback, quantitative results, and APG signoff documentation, are available in designated SharePoint folders.

Section 8.1: Outcomes Analysis

8.1.1 In-Sample Testing

IRIS is primarily a Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) system rather than a custom-trained statistical model. Therefore, traditional in-sample testing on a training dataset is not applicable in the conventional sense. The system does not require training data in the traditional machine learning context, as it utilizes pre-trained models for inference. Instead, the system's effectiveness was validated through comprehensive query testing against its knowledge base.

8.1.2 Cross-Validation

8.1.2.1 Out-of-Sample Testing

The system was tested using two distinct query sets developed specifically for validation purposes: (1) approximately 50 APG-Focused Queries provided by the Accounting Policy Group, targeting accounting policy databases, and (2) approximately 50 CFO-Focused Queries curated by the internal team to test broader CFO group databases. These queries were designed to cover common inquiry types and critical finance and accounting areas across all integrated knowledge sources, and were distinct from any examples used during system development.

8.1.2.2 Out-of-Time Testing

N/A - As IRIS is not a time-series forecasting model, traditional out-of-time testing comparing predicted versus actual values over a time period not used in development is not applicable.

8.1.2.3 Out-of-Sample Out-of-Time Testing

N/A - As noted above, IRIS does not utilize time series forecasting methods or require validation across both new samples and time periods.

8.1.3 Initial and Ongoing Backtesting

Initial testing was conducted through a comprehensive, iterative process wherein the system's responses were compared with expected answers prepared by APG subject matter experts. The system demonstrated strong performance across all non-APG databases with an overall performance score of 89.6%, database selection accuracy of 99.4%, document selection accuracy of 97.7%, and answer accuracy of 94.2%.

For APG databases, the evaluation process involved direct feedback and signoff rather than numerical scoring, with final signoff received for all 50 APG test questions. Plans for ongoing monitoring include periodic retesting of the query dataset to identify potential degradation in performance as new documents are integrated.

Section 8.2 Benchmarking Analysis

As IRIS represents the first implementation of an AI assistant for finance and accounting policy guidance at RBC, direct internal benchmarking against alternative models was not performed. However, the performance of the key components was assessed against the following benchmarks:

  • GPT-4o and GPT-4o-mini models were evaluated based on OpenAI's published benchmark results for instruction following and reasoning tasks
  • Text-embedding-3-large model was benchmarked against OpenAI's published statistics for semantic similarity matching
  • Qwen2 Vision model's performance was compared to published benchmark statistics for visual document processing

These benchmarks provided reasonable confidence that the selected models would meet the system requirements. The actual implementation was then validated through the comprehensive query testing described above.

Section 8.3 Sensitivity Analysis

Sensitivity testing was conducted to evaluate the system's performance under various conditions:

  • Query Variation Sensitivity: The system was tested with multiple phrasings of similar queries to assess its robustness to question formulation differences. Results showed consistent database and document selection regardless of phrasing variations, with only minor differences in response formatting.
  • Document Source Sensitivity: Testing included queries that would retrieve information from multiple document sources to assess the system's ability to synthesize information across databases. The system demonstrated effective synthesis capabilities after implementing parallel database querying.
  • Model Parameter Sensitivity: Various model temperature settings were tested to find the optimal balance between creativity and consistency. A temperature of 0.0 was selected for database selection and document retrieval to ensure deterministic behavior, while a slightly higher temperature (0.2) was chosen for response generation to allow for appropriate synthesis of information.

This sensitivity analysis informed system configuration choices and helped identify areas requiring architectural improvements, such as parallel database querying.

Section 8.4 Stress Testing

N/A - Traditional stress testing as described in the guidelines is not applicable to this type of model. IRIS is not used for market risk regulatory capital calculations, counterparty credit risk regulatory capital calculations, or internal market risk stress testing scenarios. The system is a research and information tool designed to provide guidance on finance and accounting policies.

However, the system was tested for appropriate behavior under challenging conditions through negative testing, which confirmed that the system:

  • Correctly identifies queries outside its knowledge domain and provides appropriate responses
  • Includes appropriate disclaimers when queries cannot be answered with confidence
  • Maintains proper boundaries regarding financial advice and regulatory guidance
  • Consistently includes required disclaimer statements in messages

Section 8.5 Other Tests

Additional tests were conducted to validate specific aspects of the system:

8.5.1 Retrieval Method Validation

Each of the three primary retrieval methods was validated for its specific use case through the iterative testing process:

  • Catalog Search - Small: Demonstrated effectiveness for standard documents and Excel/Vision pipelines
  • Catalog Search - Large: Proved appropriate for larger documents with distinct sections
  • Semantic Search - Large: Confirmed effectiveness for very large reference documents

8.5.2 User Interface Testing

Several issues related to the user interface were identified and addressed:

  • Modified the UI code to allow scrolling during response streaming while maintaining auto-scroll after completion
  • Clarified that thumbs up/down feedback controls were planned for the production implementation
  • Implemented multi-pass vision preprocessing to extract detailed content from graphical elements

8.5.3 Identified Issues and Resolutions

Through the iterative testing process, several performance issues were identified and addressed:

  • Unsolicited IFRS vs. GAAP Comparisons: Enhanced prompt instructions to focus only on requested standards
  • Incomplete Answers Due to Sequential Database Processing: Implemented parallel database querying
  • Overly Verbose Response Format: Eliminated internal "thinking" steps from display
  • Incorrect Handling of Finance-Related Queries: Updated the Router agent to explicitly route all finance-related queries to the research path
  • Missing Critical Information: Enhanced prompts for subagents to accurately extract specific conditions or criteria
  • Unnecessary Detail in Responses: Implemented adaptive synthesis logic to match response scope to user requests
  • Inconsistent Spacing/Formatting: Enhanced formatting instructions with explicit guidelines
  • Incorrect Labeling of Source Authority: Updated source descriptions to clearly label different types of guidance

8.6 Testing Limitations

It is important to acknowledge several limitations in the testing approach:

  • Formal blind testing with independent evaluators was not conducted due to resource constraints; testing relied on the same teams involved in development
  • Extensive statistical validation was not performed, given the system's nature as a RAG implementation rather than a custom-trained model
  • Alternative RAG architectures or methods were not explicitly tested as comparisons
  • Extensive load or stress testing to identify performance boundaries was not conducted, though basic functionality was confirmed
  • Testing relied on available subject matter experts rather than a formal, diverse panel of evaluators
  • Testing has been conducted primarily in the Dataiku environment, with full Maven integration testing pending

These limitations are considered acceptable given the nature of the project and its intended use as an internal tool with human oversight.

8.7 Testing Conclusions

The testing conducted for the IRIS system provides reasonable confidence in its ability to serve its intended purpose. The key strengths demonstrated through testing include:

  • Core Functionality: Effective retrieval of relevant information and generation of appropriate responses (99.4% database selection, 97.7% document selection, 94.2% answer accuracy)
  • Knowledge Boundaries: Appropriate identification of when information is outside its knowledge base
  • Response Quality: Acceptable level for production use, evidenced by formal APG signoff and an overall performance score of 89.6%
  • Document Integration: Ability to incorporate new documents through the database refresh process

Based on the testing results, the IRIS system is considered suitable for deployment as a finance and accounting policy guidance tool, with the understanding that human oversight remains essential for verifying responses on critical matters. The feedback collection mechanism and ongoing monitoring will support continuous improvement as the system is used in practice.
